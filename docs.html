What ATS is

ATS is a per-user algo-trading platform for Indian retail equity markets. It plugs into your Zerodha account (read-only by default), runs a momentum scanner across your watchlist, and asks an AI model you pay for directly (BYOK — bring your own key) to validate each signal before you act.

It is advisory only. ATS never executes a trade on your behalf without your explicit confirmation. The AI critic suggests; you decide; your broker executes.

💡 If you're just exploring, sign up, add your Zerodha key in read-only mode, and run the paper-trading sandbox. No real money is at risk.

Sign up & first login

  1. Visit ats.rajasekarselvam.com and click Sign up.
  2. Enter your email + a password. A verification email arrives within ~30 seconds.
  3. Click the verification link. You'll land on the onboarding wizard.
  4. Complete the wizard: timezone, default watchlist (skip if unsure), preferred currency display.

The first account on a fresh ATS install is automatically promoted to admin. There's exactly one user today (you). Multi-user beta begins when Tier 2 readiness is met.

Connect your broker

ATS currently supports Zerodha (Kite Connect) in production, with Dhan and AngelOne adapters available but not yet promoted to live.

  1. Go to Brokers in the sidebar.
  2. Click Connect Zerodha. The 3-step wizard prompts for your Kite API key + secret.
  3. The first OAuth login happens in a browser window. ATS stores the access token sealed in libsodium (only your password-derived key can open it).
  4. The daily auto-reauth cron fires at 05:45 IST so your token is fresh before market open.
⚠ You need your own Kite Connect developer account (₹2000/month from Zerodha). ATS doesn't proxy your trades through a shared key.

Add an AI provider key

ATS uses your own API key for every AI call. The key is sealed with libsodium before being written to the database; the cleartext is never logged or sent anywhere except the provider you configured.

  1. Open AI providers in the sidebar (or /#ai-keys).
  2. Generate a key in your provider's console:
    • Claude (Anthropic) — recommended primary
    • OpenAI — used as fallback
    • Google Gemini — cheapest tier
  3. Paste the key into the provider's card on the page, click Test to verify it works, then Save.

You can add 1, 2, or 3 keys. The auto-router uses your most-preferred available provider for each workflow; if your top pick is missing, it falls through to the next.

AI Signals page

The momentum scanner runs daily at 15:35 IST and inspects every symbol in your watchlist for these technical signals:

  • RSI_OVERSOLD — 14-day RSI < 30 (potential buy zone)
  • RSI_OVERBOUGHT — 14-day RSI > 70 (potential sell zone)
  • EMA20_CROSS_UP — close crossed above 20-day EMA (trend up)
  • EMA20_CROSS_DOWN — close crossed below 20-day EMA (trend down)

Each signal appears as a card. Click the Critique button to get an AI verdict: agree / caution / reject, with confidence score, summary, key risks, and a suggested next step. Critiques are cached per signal so re-clicking the same card returns instantly at ₹0.

💡 The scanner already skips symbols on the NSE surveillance lists (T2T, GSM stage 2+). See Surveillance gate.

Auto-router & modes

You don't pick a model. ATS routes every AI call automatically based on the workflow + your mode setting.

  • Quality — always the best model in the family. Highest cost, highest signal quality.
  • Balanced (default) — quality-first defaults. Critique + reviews stay on Sonnet even though it's not the cheapest. Explainers drop to Haiku.
  • Economy — cheapest viable model per workflow. Fastest + lowest cost; signal quality may drop on STRONG workflows.

The router prefers Anthropic when you have a Claude key, falls through to OpenAI then Gemini for each call. See exactly what gets picked in the What ATS picks for each workflow table on the AI providers page.

Paper trading

Paper mode is your sandbox. Trades execute against live market prices (from your Kite WebSocket feed) but no real money moves. Cash, positions, P&L all behave as if real.

  • Set your initial paper capital on the Paper screen (default ₹10L).
  • Pick a strategy → bind it to a symbol → enable auto-runner if you want it to fire on signals.
  • Watch the P&L attribution tab to see which strategies actually contributed.

Recommendation: run a strategy in paper for 30 days minimum before promoting it to live, with the win-rate gate at ≥55% and the monthly-review headline corroborating the numbers.

Surveillance gate

The scanner automatically excludes symbols that NSE has placed under surveillance restrictions. These include:

  • T2T (Trade-to-Trade) — series BE/BZ/BL/ST/IV/SZ. Cash-only, no intraday, no margin.
  • GSM (Graded Surveillance Measure) Stage II and above. Indicates circular-trading suspicion or low-float pump-and-dump risk.

Today the gate blocks ~500 symbols. The data refreshes every hour from archives.nseindia.com/content/equities/sec_list.csv. You'll see them skipped in the scanner audit log with the gate reason.

Spend cap

Every account has a daily spend cap (default ₹50/day). When the cap is hit, new AI calls return spend_cap_exceeded (HTTP 429) until midnight IST. The cap protects you from runaway costs if a script gets stuck in a loop.

  • Editable on the AI providers card (range ₹0 – ₹5,000).
  • Set to ₹0 to disable all AI calls entirely without removing your keys.
  • The cap is enforced before the LLM call is made — blocked attempts are logged with status=blocked_by_cap at ₹0 so you can audit them in the cost breakdown.

SEBI & advisory

ATS is not registered with SEBI as a Research Analyst or Investment Adviser. Every AI-generated output renders the standard advisory disclaimer:

Advisory only. Not investment advice. All AI-generated views are for educational use; you remain solely responsible for any trade you place. Past performance does not predict future results.

The platform also enforces a 2FA confirm-before-trade step on live orders. The AI critic can suggest a trade but cannot place one — that gate is structural, not configurable.

Privacy & data

ATS stores the minimum needed to run your account:

  • Your email + bcrypt-hashed password
  • Sealed broker access tokens (libsodium-encrypted at rest)
  • Sealed AI provider API keys (same vault)
  • Paper trade history + P&L attribution
  • AI call audit log (workflow, provider, model, token counts, cost, thumb feedback)
  • Append-only audit trail of key events

What ATS does not store: real trade execution data (that lives on your broker), your raw account balance, anything you put into a freeform notes field. AI prompts include your symbol + signal context, but never your password, broker secrets, or AI key cleartext.

Backups & DR

Nightly at 02:30 UTC (08:00 IST), a cron pushes the SQLite database + sealed token directory + rotated audit logs to Google Drive via rclone. Recovery point objective is 24 hours; recovery time objective is <1 hour.

The DR test script (/opt/ats/scripts/dr-restore-test.sh --notify) restores from the latest snapshot into a scratch directory and verifies integrity. The result is recorded in /api/health-deep under drLastTestAgo; if it's been >30 days since a successful test, drStale: true warns operators.

FAQ

Why do I need to bring my own AI key?

So ATS never sits between you and a high-cost provider. You see your real spend on the provider's bill, you control your spend cap, and there's no surprise markup. ATS itself is free (single user today; pricing for multi-user beta TBD).

Why is the spend cap ₹50/day default?

It covers a normal day of personal trading (~10-20 critiques + 1 analyze + occasional explainer). If you're scanning a 200-symbol watchlist with all critiques on Sonnet, raise it; if you only use ATS for a couple of weekly reviews, lower it.

Can the AI place a trade for me?

No. The architecture makes this impossible — the AI critic only emits structured JSON verdicts. Trade placement goes through a separate path that requires a 2FA confirmation per trade.

Why does the scanner skip some of my watchlist symbols?

Most likely they're on the NSE surveillance lists (T2T / GSM). Check the scanner audit log; each skip is recorded with the gate reason. You can still trade these on your broker — ATS just refuses to suggest them.

What happens if Kite API is down?

The status page shows it. The scanner skips that run. Cached prices stay frozen until the next successful tick. You'll see a yellow banner on the dashboard.

Can I export my data?

Yes — Account → Export downloads a JSON of your preferences, watchlist, paper trades, and AI call history. Sealed credentials are NOT exported (they wouldn't be useful without the master key).

Where do I read the audit trail?

Compliance → Audit log shows the WORM-style append-only trail of every key action: logins, broker connects, AI calls, paper trades, scanner runs. Auto-rotated daily; 7 days kept on disk + archived forever to GDrive.
